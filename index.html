What Kind of Damage Can Dangerous Trees Cause?

A damaged, leaning, dead, or overgrown tree can become a serious safety risk. Trees are extremely heavy, and when a trunk, limb, or root system fails, the damage can be sudden and expensive.

  • Roof damage from falling limbs or full tree failure
  • Broken fences, sheds, garages, and decks
  • Vehicle damage from falling branches
  • Blocked driveways and roads after storms
  • Power outages caused by limbs contacting utility lines
  • Foundation, sidewalk, and driveway damage from aggressive root growth
  • Injury risk to family members, pets, neighbors, or visitors

High winds, saturated soil, trunk rot, insect damage, and root problems can all increase the chance of tree failure. If a tree already looks unstable, waiting too long can turn a manageable job into an emergency.
