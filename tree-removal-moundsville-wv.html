When a Tree Needs to Go

You probably should not keep “waiting to see what happens” if the tree is:

  • Leaning more than it used to
  • Dropping large branches
  • Dead or mostly dead
  • Cracked or split
  • Too close to the house or garage
  • Damaged after a storm
  • Interfering with access, parking, or other work on the property

A lot of tree problems look minor right up until they cave in a roof, smash a fence, or block a driveway at the worst possible time. That is usually how these things like to introduce themselves.

What Affects Tree Removal Cost?

Tree Size

Taller and thicker trees usually take more time, labor, and equipment.

Location

Trees close to homes, decks, garages, power lines, or fences are more difficult to remove safely.

Access

Easy access can make a job simpler. Tight spaces, hills, and obstacles usually make it harder.

Condition

Dead, split, storm-damaged, or unstable trees can require more careful handling.

Every tree job is different. The cheapest tree to remove is the one that is easy to access, away from structures, and not falling apart. The expensive ones are the problem children hanging over roofs and acting like physics is optional.
