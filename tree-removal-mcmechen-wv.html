Signs a Tree May Need Removed

  • The tree is leaning more than it used to
  • Large branches are breaking off
  • The trunk is split or cracked
  • The tree is dead or mostly dead
  • It is too close to the house, garage, or utility lines
  • Storm damage has weakened the structure
  • Roots or trunk problems make the tree unstable

Trees usually give warnings before they fail. The problem is a lot of people ignore them until the tree decides to audition for demolition work.

What Affects Tree Removal Cost?

Tree Size

Larger trees usually require more labor, more time, and more careful cutting.

Location

Trees near structures, fences, roofs, or power lines are usually more difficult and risky to remove.

Access

Open access can simplify the job. Tight yards, hills, and obstacles usually increase difficulty.

Tree Condition

Dead, brittle, cracked, or storm-damaged trees often require more careful handling during removal.

Every tree removal job is different. A tree standing in an open yard is one thing. A giant half-dead tree leaning toward a roof like it has unfinished business is another.
